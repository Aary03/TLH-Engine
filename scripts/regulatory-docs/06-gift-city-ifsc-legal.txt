PART 1: WHAT IS GIFT CITY IFSC?
=============================================================================

Gujarat International Finance Tec-City (GIFT City) is India's first operational
International Financial Services Centre (IFSC), located in Gandhinagar, Gujarat.

GOVERNING STATUTE: International Financial Services Centres Authority (IFSCA) Act, 2019
- IFSCA is the unified regulator for ALL financial services in IFSC
- Replaced the earlier multi-regulator framework (RBI, SEBI, IRDAI separately)
- IFSCA Fund Management Regulations 2022 (updated 2024) govern fund management

IFSCA was established to develop and regulate:
- Banking, capital markets, insurance in IFSC
- Fund management including mutual funds, AIF, PMS
- Fintech, global in-house centres, ship/aircraft leasing

KEY LEGAL FICTION: Despite being physically in India, GIFT City IFSC is treated as:
- "Outside India" for FEMA purposes (Section 2(i), FEMA 1999)
- "Outside India" for TCS purposes (Section 206C(1G) IT Act, post-2020 amendment)
- A "Special Economic Zone" under SEZ Act 2005
- A "Unit" in IFSC is deemed to be a "person resident outside India" under FEMA

CONSEQUENCE: Indian residents investing in GIFT City IFSC funds must use LRS,
and the transactions are treated as cross-border for regulatory purposes.

PART 3: TAX INCENTIVES FOR IFSC ENTITIES (NOT FOR INVESTORS)
=============================================================================

These are tax benefits available to the FUND MANAGEMENT ENTITY (FME) and FUND itself,
NOT to the Indian resident investor. Critical to distinguish.

SECTION 80LA (Income Tax Act):
- 100% deduction on income earned for first 10 consecutive years (out of 15 years)
- Applicable to IFSC units: banking, insurance, fund management entities
- Effective corporate tax rate on fund income = ~0% for first 10 years

SECTION 10(4D) (Income Tax Act):
- Complete exemption for income of an investment fund set up in IFSC
- This applies at the FUND level, NOT at the investor level

SECTION 10(23FF):
- Exemption for income of FPIs from transfer of securities in IFSC
- Applicable to overseas investors investing IN GIFT City

STT (Securities Transaction Tax): ZERO in IFSC
- No STT on any transaction in IFSC — buying, selling, switching funds
- vs 0.001% STT on MF equity units in regular market
- For large portfolios this is material: ₹10Cr sell = ₹10,000 STT saved

STAMP DUTY: ZERO in IFSC
- No stamp duty on instrument execution in IFSC
- Maharashtra/Gujarat stamp duty does not apply

GST: ZERO on fund management services within IFSC
- Management fees, advisory fees charged by IFSC fund manager = 0% GST
- vs 18% GST on domestic fund management fees

Customs duty, import duty: Various exemptions under SEZ Act 2005

PART 4: TAX TREATMENT FOR INDIAN RESIDENT INVESTORS
=============================================================================

THIS IS THE SECTION THAT GOVERNS HOW INVESTORS PAY TAX — NOT THE FUND.

HISTORICAL NOTE — SECTION 50AA (the dark period):
- Budget 2023 (FY 2023-24 and 2024-25): IFSC funds classified as "specified mutual funds"
- Section 50AA: ALL gains taxed as STCG at slab rate regardless of holding period
- This made IFSC funds tax-inefficient for Indian investors for 2 years
- Budget 2025: Section 50AA applicability removed for IFSC/overseas funds investing
  in overseas assets. Standard capital gains treatment restored.

CURRENT TREATMENT (FY 2025-26 ONWARDS):

Classification test:
- IFSC fund investing primarily in overseas equities/ETFs = NON-EQUITY fund
  (fails 65% domestic equity test)
- Therefore: Section 112 rates apply (NOT Section 111A)

LTCG (Long-Term Capital Gains):
- Holding period: MORE THAN 24 MONTHS (730 days) — Section 2(42A)
- Rate: 12.5% FLAT under Section 112
- Surcharge: CAPPED AT 15% under proviso to Section 112(1)
- Health and Education Cess: 4%
- Maximum effective rate: 12.5% × 1.15 × 1.04 = 14.95%
- No indexation benefit (Budget 2024 removed indexation for all assets)
- Rs. 1.25 lakh exemption: NOT APPLICABLE (only for domestic listed equity with STT)

STCG (Short-Term Capital Gains):
- Holding period: 24 MONTHS OR LESS (730 days or less)
- Rate: At investor's applicable income tax SLAB RATE
- Surcharge: NO CAP — full surcharge at applicable rate
- Health and Education Cess: 4%
- Maximum (old regime, above Rs 5Cr income): 30% × 1.37 × 1.04 = 42.744%
- Maximum (new regime, above Rs 5Cr): 30% × 1.25 × 1.04 = 39%

THE CRITICAL SPREAD:
- STCG max rate: 42.744%
- LTCG max rate: 14.95%
- Spread: 27.794 percentage points
- Per Rs 1 crore gain: Rs 27.79 lakh MORE tax if sold before 730 days vs after

CURRENCY: Gains computed in INR using exchange rate on transaction date
- SBI TTBR (Telegraphic Transfer Buying Rate) on date of sale
- Cost basis also converted at SBI TTBR on date of purchase
- Both rates locked at time of transaction

PART 5: FEMA TREATMENT AND LRS IMPLICATIONS
=============================================================================

FEMA NOTIFICATION: FEMA 1(R)/2000-RB, AP DIR Circular No. 64/2006
- IFSC units are treated as "persons resident outside India" per FEMA definitions
- Investment in IFSC funds = Capital account transaction under FEMA
- Must be routed through Authorised Dealer banks
- LRS limits apply: USD 250,000 per resident individual per financial year

FORM A2: Must be filed for every remittance to IFSC
FORM 15CA/15CB: Required for TCS compliance (bank handles this)

RE-INVESTMENT WITHIN IFSC:
- Once remitted, gains/income can be REINVESTED within IFSC without fresh LRS
- This is the GIFT City advantage: Compound within IFSC without each reinvestment
  counting against LRS limits
- Only INITIAL remittance and FINAL repatriation count

REPATRIATION: No restriction on repatriating principal + gains back to India
- Subject to FEMA/AD bank procedures
- No approval needed from RBI for standard redemptions

PART 6: US ESTATE TAX — THE GIFT CITY STRUCTURAL ADVANTAGE
=============================================================================

US ESTATE TAX PROBLEM FOR INDIANS:
- US estate tax: up to 40% on death
- Applies to "US-situs assets" held by Non-Resident Aliens (NRAs)
- NRA exemption: Only $60,000 (vs ~$13.99 million for US citizens)
- NO India-US estate tax treaty exists
- US-situs assets include: US stocks, US-domiciled ETFs (VOO, VTI, SPY, QQQ)
  — regardless of WHERE the brokerage account is held

WHAT COUNTS AS US-SITUS:
- Shares in US corporations
- US government bonds
- US real estate
- US-domiciled ETFs (iShares S&P 500 US-listed, Vanguard US-listed)

WHAT IS NOT US-SITUS:
- Ireland-domiciled ETFs (iShares Core S&P 500 UCITS, Vanguard FTSE All World UCITS)
- Luxembourg-domiciled funds
- Indian fund units (even if they invest in US stocks)

THE GIFT CITY SOLUTION:
Structure: Indian investor → GIFT City IFSC fund → Ireland UCITS ETF → US stocks

Step 1: Indian investor holds UNITS of GIFT City IFSC fund
        → NOT a US-situs asset → ZERO estate tax exposure
Step 2: GIFT City fund holds Ireland UCITS ETFs
        → Ireland-domiciled → NOT US-situs → ZERO estate tax exposure
Step 3: Ireland UCITS holds US stocks
        → At fund level, not at investor level

RESULT: Complete elimination of US estate tax exposure for Indian investors.

DIVIDEND WITHHOLDING TAX BENEFIT:
- Indian investor directly in US stocks: 25% WHT (India-US DTAA rate)
- Irish UCITS in US stocks: 15% WHT (US-Ireland tax treaty — more favorable)
- Annual saving: 10% on all US dividends, compounding over decades

PART 7: SCHEDULE FA, FSI, TR — REPORTING OBLIGATIONS
=============================================================================

SCHEDULE FA (Foreign Assets) IN ITR:
MANDATORY for Indian residents holding IFSC fund units.
Reason: Despite physical location in India, IFSC entities = "outside India" under FEMA.

What to report:
- Country code: IN (India, since GIFT City is technically in India)
- Name of institution (AMC name + IFSC designation)
- Account/folio number
- Opening balance (January 1) in USD and INR
- Peak balance during year
- Closing balance (December 31)
- INR equivalent at SBI TTBR

Reporting period: CALENDAR YEAR (January 1 to December 31)
This is different from Indian financial year (April 1 to March 31)

ITR FORMS: Must use ITR-2 or ITR-3 (NOT ITR-1 or ITR-4 which lack Schedule FA)

BLACK MONEY ACT, 2015 — PENALTY STRUCTURE:
Section 42: Penalty of Rs 10 LAKH per year for non-disclosure of foreign assets
Section 50: Prosecution: 6 months to 7 years imprisonment for willful non-disclosure
CRS/FATCA: India receives data from 100+ countries — IT Dept will independently know

Budget 2024 relief: No penalty for non-immovable foreign assets below aggregate Rs 20 lakh

SCHEDULE FSI (Foreign Source Income):
- All income from IFSC: dividends, interest, capital gains
- Each income item: country, amount in foreign currency, INR equivalent, foreign tax paid

FORM 67 (Foreign Tax Credit):
- File electronically to claim FTC under Section 90/91
- Deadline: March 31 of Assessment Year (Section 139(1) read with CBDT Notification 100/2022)
- For FY 2025-26 gains: Form 67 by March 31, 2027
- Currency conversion: SBI TTBR on last day of preceding month
- FTC = min(foreign tax paid, Indian tax on same income)

PART 9: SECTION 94(8) — BONUS STRIPPING — IFSC CONTEXT
=============================================================================

Section 94(8) is the ONLY wash-sale-like rule in India's tax law.

CONDITIONS FOR LOSS DISALLOWANCE:
1. MF units acquired within 3 months BEFORE the bonus record date
2. Bonus units received on record date
3. ORIGINAL (not bonus) units sold at a LOSS within 9 months AFTER record date

EFFECT: Loss on original units is ignored; deemed to be cost of bonus units.

PRACTICAL IMPACT FOR GIFT CITY FUNDS:
- IFSC funds rarely declare bonus units (more common in domestic funds)
- If an IFSC fund does declare bonus: check the exact dates
- Example: If record date = September 15, 2025:
  - Units acquired before June 15, 2025 (3 months prior) = SAFE to harvest
  - Units acquired June 15 – September 15, 2025 = Section 94(8) risk
  - Loss can be harvested AFTER June 15, 2026 (9 months after record date)

THIS IS THE ONLY RESTRICTION ON TLH IN INDIA. All other harvesting is unrestricted.

PART 10: IFSCA INVESTOR PROTECTIONS
=============================================================================

IFSCA CIRCULAR ON INVESTOR GRIEVANCES:
- FME must have a designated compliance officer
- IFSCA operates an investor protection fund
- Dispute resolution through IFSCA's investor grievance mechanism

NAV PUBLICATION:
- Daily NAV publication mandatory for retail schemes
- Available on IFSCA website and AMC websites
- No AMFI listing (unlike domestic MF NAVs on amfiindia.com)
- Data sourced from individual AMC websites/investor portals

AUDIT REQUIREMENTS:
- Annual audit by Big4 or equivalent firm
- SOC 2 or equivalent operational controls
- Quarterly portfolio disclosures

MINIMUM INVESTMENT:
- Retail Schemes: No minimum (similar to domestic MF)
- Restricted Schemes (AIF equivalent): USD 150,000 minimum per investor
- Venture Capital Schemes: USD 250,000 minimum

REPATRIATION: No lock-in on redemptions for retail schemes (unlike restricted schemes)
Typically T+3 settlement for redemptions

PART 11: PRACTICAL TLH RULES UNDER IFSCA FRAMEWORK
=============================================================================

Since India has no wash sale rules:
1. SELL IFSC fund units at a loss → loss recognized immediately
2. REBUY same fund same day → allowed (no wash sale restriction)
3. New cost basis = rebuy price (stepped down from original)
4. Holding period restarts from date of rebuy
5. Transaction costs: typically 0.1-0.25% of trade value (no STT/GST helps)

GAAR SAFETY:
- Transaction at market prices = safe
- Both legs (sell and rebuy) at market NAV = documented commercial substance
- Keep records: broker confirmations, trade statements showing both transactions

SETTLEMENT: T+2 for IFSC ETFs; T+3 for IFSC fund units (check with specific AMC)
IMPORTANT: For year-end TLH (before March 31), ensure SALE SETTLEMENT completes
before March 31. Execute trades by approximately March 28-29 to be safe.

PART 12: KEY CBDT NOTIFICATIONS AND CIRCULARS
=============================================================================

CBDT Notification No. 100/2022:
- Form 67 filing deadline: March 31 of Assessment Year

Finance Act 2025 (Budget 2025-26 amendments):
- TCS threshold raised from Rs 7 lakh to Rs 10 lakh per PAN per FY
- Surcharge cap of 15% on LTCG maintained (not changed)
- Section 50AA applicability removed for overseas asset funds (restoring normal CG treatment)

Finance Act 2024 (Budget 2024-25):
- LTCG rate changed from 20% (with indexation) to 12.5% flat (no indexation)
- Holding period for non-equity funds: 24 months (changed from 36 months)
- Surcharge on capital gains maintained at 15% cap for Section 112

CBDT Circular on IFSC tax treatment (April 2024):
- Clarification that Section 10(4D) exemption applies at fund level, not investor level
- Indian resident investors in IFSC funds cannot claim 10(4D) exemption
- Capital gains from IFSC fund units by Indian residents = normal CG treatment

SEBI-IFSCA MOU (2021):
- Delineation of regulatory jurisdiction
- IFSCA has exclusive jurisdiction over entities in IFSC
- Indian domestic entities (investing through LRS into IFSC) remain under SEBI oversight
