CAPITAL GAINS TAXATION ON IFSC/OVERSEAS FUNDS
Income Tax Act 1961 — Sections 112, 111A, 48
Applicable: FY 2025-26 and onwards

=============================================================================
SECTION 1: CLASSIFICATION OF IFSC/OVERSEAS FUNDS
=============================================================================

GIFT City IFSC funds investing in overseas equities are classified as 
NON-EQUITY/OVERSEAS FUNDS because they fail the 65% domestic equity threshold 
required for "equity-oriented" fund status.

Classification test:
- If fund invests ≥65% in domestic listed equity: Equity fund (Sec 111A rates)
- If fund invests <65% in domestic equity (including overseas/global funds): Non-equity fund (Sec 112 rates)

GIFT City IFSC funds typically invest in:
- Ireland-domiciled UCITS ETFs (which invest in US/global equities)
- Direct overseas equities
Result: Always classified as non-equity funds

IMPORTANT HISTORICAL NOTE:
- FY 2023-24 and FY 2024-25: These funds were treated as "specified mutual funds" 
  under Section 50AA — gains taxed as STCG at slab rate regardless of holding period
- FY 2025-26 ONWARDS: Section 50AA treatment ended. Standard STCG/LTCG treatment applies.

=============================================================================
SECTION 2: APPLICABLE TAX RATES (FY 2025-26)
=============================================================================

LONG-TERM CAPITAL GAINS (LTCG):
- Section: 112 of the Income Tax Act
- Rate: 12.5% FLAT (no indexation available)
- Holding period: MORE THAN 24 MONTHS (730 days)
- Surcharge: CAPPED at 15% (regardless of income level)
- Health and Education Cess: 4%
- Effective maximum rate: 12.5% × (1 + 15%) × (1 + 4%) = 14.95%
- Rs. 1.25 lakh annual exemption: NOT APPLICABLE (only for domestic listed equity with STT)
- Indexation benefit: NOT AVAILABLE

SHORT-TERM CAPITAL GAINS (STCG):
- Section: Taxed at investor's income slab rate
- Rate: At applicable income tax slab rate
- Holding period: UP TO 24 MONTHS (≤730 days)
- Surcharge: NO CAP — full surcharge applies based on income
- Health and Education Cess: 4%

=============================================================================
SECTION 3: EFFECTIVE RATE TABLE
=============================================================================

LTCG EFFECTIVE RATES (12.5% base, 15% surcharge cap, 4% cess):
Income Level          | Effective LTCG Rate
Up to Rs. 50 lakh     | 12.5% × 1.00 × 1.04 = 13.00%
Rs. 50L – Rs. 1Cr     | 12.5% × 1.10 × 1.04 = 14.30%
Rs. 1Cr – Rs. 2Cr     | 12.5% × 1.15 × 1.04 = 14.95%
Rs. 2Cr – Rs. 5Cr     | 12.5% × 1.15 × 1.04 = 14.95% (surcharge capped at 15%)
Above Rs. 5Cr         | 12.5% × 1.15 × 1.04 = 14.95% (surcharge capped at 15%)

The 15% surcharge cap on LTCG is a crucial benefit under Sec 112.
Normally, income above Rs. 5Cr attracts 37% surcharge (old regime) or 25% (new).
But for Section 112 LTCG, the surcharge is capped at 15%.

STCG EFFECTIVE RATES (slab rate, NO surcharge cap, 4% cess):
Income Level          | Slab Rate | Surcharge | STCG Effective Rate
Up to Rs. 50 lakh     | 30%       | 0%        | 30% × 1.04 = 31.20%
Rs. 50L – Rs. 1Cr     | 30%       | 10%       | 30% × 1.10 × 1.04 = 34.32%
Rs. 1Cr – Rs. 2Cr     | 30%       | 15%       | 30% × 1.15 × 1.04 = 35.88%
Rs. 2Cr – Rs. 5Cr     | 30%       | 25%       | 30% × 1.25 × 1.04 = 39.00%
Above Rs. 5Cr (old)   | 30%       | 37%       | 30% × 1.37 × 1.04 = 42.744%
Above Rs. 5Cr (new)   | 30%       | 25%       | 30% × 1.25 × 1.04 = 39.00%

=============================================================================
SECTION 4: KEY TAX PLANNING IMPLICATIONS
=============================================================================

THE SPREAD: At income above Rs. 5Cr (old regime):
- STCG rate: 42.74%
- LTCG rate: 14.95%
- Spread: 27.79 percentage points

This enormous spread means:
1. Holding for 730+ days saves up to 27.79% on gains
2. Harvesting STCL (short-term capital losses) is worth up to 42.74% per rupee
3. LTCL (long-term capital losses) is worth only 14.95% per rupee

STCL SUPERIOR TO LTCL: A short-term capital loss is almost always more valuable
than an equivalent long-term capital loss because:
- STCL can offset BOTH STCG (at 42.74%) AND LTCG (at 14.95%)
- LTCL can ONLY offset LTCG (at 14.95%)

WHEN TO TAKE STCG: Generally avoid unless you have STCL to offset it.
The 42.74% tax on STCG significantly erodes returns.

COST BASIS: New regime vs Old regime implications:
- New regime: Max STCG rate = 39% (no benefit of old regime deductions)
- Old regime: Max STCG rate = 42.74% (but deductions reduce taxable income)

=============================================================================
SECTION 5: HOLDING PERIOD CALCULATION
=============================================================================

For mutual funds and ETFs:
- Purchase date: date of allotment of units
- Sale date: date of redemption/sale
- Period: computed in days (730 days = long-term threshold)
- 730 days = exactly 2 years

Example: Bought on June 15, 2024 → held until June 14, 2026 = 730 days = STCG
         Bought on June 15, 2024 → held until June 15, 2026 = 731 days = LTCG

IMPORTANT: The 24-month threshold is 730 days, NOT simply "after 2 years" in calendar terms.
Leap years affect the exact date. Always count in actual days.
