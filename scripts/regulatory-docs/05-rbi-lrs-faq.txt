RBI OFFICIAL FAQ — LIBERALISED REMITTANCE SCHEME (LRS)
Reserve Bank of India
Updated: April 06, 2023
Source: rbi.org.in — Banker and Debt Manager to Government

Legal Framework: Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999 (FEMA), in force from June 1, 2000.
All foreign exchange transactions by residents are either current or capital account transactions under FEMA.

=============================================================================
Q1. What is the Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS) of USD 2,50,000?
=============================================================================

Under the Liberalised Remittance Scheme, all resident individuals, including minors, are allowed to
freely remit up to USD 2,50,000 per financial year (April – March) for any permissible current or
capital account transaction or a combination of both.

Resident individuals can avail foreign exchange facility for purposes mentioned in Para 1 of Schedule III
of FEM (CAT) Amendment Rules 2015, dated May 26, 2015, within the limit of USD 2,50,000 only.

The Scheme was introduced on February 4, 2004, with a limit of USD 25,000.
The LRS limit has been revised in stages consistent with prevailing macro and micro economic conditions.

In case of remitter being a minor, the LRS declaration form must be countersigned by the minor's
natural guardian.

The Scheme is NOT available to: corporates, partnership firms, HUF, Trusts etc.

=============================================================================
Q2. What are the prohibited items under the Scheme?
=============================================================================

The remittance facility under the Scheme is NOT available for:

1. Remittance for any purpose specifically prohibited under Schedule-I (lottery tickets, proscribed
   magazines, etc.) or any item restricted under Schedule II of Foreign Exchange Management
   (Current Account Transactions) Rules, 2000.

2. Remittance from India for margins or margin calls to overseas exchanges / overseas counterparty.

3. Remittances for purchase of FCCBs issued by Indian companies in the overseas secondary market.

4. Remittance for trading in foreign exchange abroad.

5. Capital account remittances, directly or indirectly, to countries identified by FATF as
   "non-cooperative countries and territories".

6. Remittances directly or indirectly to individuals and entities identified as posing significant
   risk of committing acts of terrorism.

7. Gifting by a resident to another resident, in foreign currency, for credit of the latter's
   foreign currency account held abroad under LRS.

=============================================================================
Q3. What purposes can resident individuals use LRS for?
=============================================================================

Individuals can avail foreign exchange facility for the following purposes within USD 2,50,000 per FY:

- Private visits to any country (except Nepal and Bhutan)
- Gift or donation
- Going abroad for employment
- Emigration
- Maintenance of close relatives abroad
- Travel for business, or attending a conference or specialised training
- Meeting expenses for medical treatment / check-up abroad
- Studies abroad
- Any other current account transaction not covered under FEMA 1999 definition

The AD bank may undertake remittance for all residual current account transactions not prohibited
under Schedule I, II or III of FEM (CAT) Rules, 2000.

=============================================================================
Q4. Are resident individuals required to repatriate income earned on overseas investments?
=============================================================================

The investor who remitted funds under LRS can retain and reinvest the income earned from investments
made under the Scheme.

However, received/realised/unspent/unused foreign exchange, unless reinvested, shall be repatriated
and surrendered to an authorised person within 180 days from the date of receipt/realisation/purchase/
acquisition or date of return to India.

Additional repatriation requirements under Overseas Investments Rules and Regulations 2022 must also
be adhered to.

=============================================================================
Q5. Can remittances under LRS be consolidated in respect of family members?
=============================================================================

Remittances under LRS can be consolidated in respect of family members subject to each individual
family member complying with the terms and conditions of the Scheme.

IMPORTANT RESTRICTION: Clubbing is NOT permitted by other family members for capital account
transactions such as opening a bank account and investment, if they are not the co-owners/co-partners
of the investment/overseas bank account.

Remittances for acquiring immovable property outside India may be consolidated in respect of relatives
if such relatives, being persons resident in India, comply with the terms and conditions of the Scheme.

=============================================================================
Q6. Is the AD required to verify permissibility of remittances?
=============================================================================

AD will be guided by the nature of transaction as declared by the remitter in Form A2 and will certify
that the remittance is in conformity with RBI instructions.

However, the ULTIMATE RESPONSIBILITY is of the remitter to ensure compliance with FEMA rules/regulations.

=============================================================================
Q7. Is PAN mandatory for LRS remittances?
=============================================================================

YES. It is mandatory for the resident individual to provide his/her Permanent Account Number (PAN)
for ALL transactions under LRS made through Authorized Persons.

=============================================================================
Q8. Are there restrictions on frequency of remittances?
=============================================================================

There are NO restrictions on the frequency of remittances under LRS.

However, the TOTAL amount of foreign exchange purchased from or remitted through ALL sources in India
during a financial year should be within the cumulative limit of USD 2,50,000.

CRITICAL: Once a remittance is made for an amount up to USD 2,50,000 during the financial year,
a resident individual would NOT be eligible to make any further remittances under this scheme,
EVEN IF the proceeds of the investments have been brought back into the country.

=============================================================================
Q10. Does the 1-year bank account requirement apply to current account transactions?
=============================================================================

NO. The 1-year minimum bank account maintenance requirement applies ONLY to capital account transactions.

For current account transactions under Schedule III (private/business visits up to USD 250,000),
FFMCs can also process these without the 1-year requirement. However, KYC and AML rules apply.

=============================================================================
Q12. What are the requirements to be complied with by the remitter?
=============================================================================

1. The individual must designate a branch of an AD through which all capital account remittances
   under the Scheme will be made.

2. The applicant should have maintained the bank account with the bank for a minimum period of
   ONE YEAR prior to the remittance (for capital account transactions).

3. For new customers: AD should carry out due diligence and obtain bank statement for the previous
   year OR copies of latest Income Tax Assessment Order or Return filed.

4. Furnish Form A-2 regarding the purpose of remittance and declare that:
   - Funds belong to the remitter
   - Will not be used for prohibited purposes under the Scheme

=============================================================================
Q13. Can remittances be made only in US Dollars?
=============================================================================

NO. Remittances can be made in ANY freely convertible foreign currency.

=============================================================================
Q15. Are there restrictions on quality of investments under LRS?
=============================================================================

NO ratings or guidelines have been prescribed under LRS of USD 2,50,000 on the quality of
investment an individual can make.

However, the individual investor is expected to exercise due diligence and investments shall be
in accordance with Overseas Investment Rules and Regulations, 2022 and directions thereunder.

=============================================================================
Q22. Remittance by sole proprietor under LRS
=============================================================================

In a sole proprietorship, there is NO legal distinction between the individual/owner.

If a sole proprietor remits USD 250,000 in their individual capacity, they CANNOT remit another
USD 250,000 as owner of the sole proprietorship — there is no legal distinction.

=============================================================================
Q25. Can a resident make a rupee loan to NRI/PIO relative?
=============================================================================

YES, subject to conditions:
(i) Loan must be free of interest; minimum maturity of one year
(ii) Loan amount must be within overall LRS limit of USD 2,50,000 per FY
(iii) Loan shall be utilised for meeting borrower's personal requirements or own business in India
(iv) Loan shall NOT be used for: chit fund, Nidhi Company, agricultural/plantation/real estate,
     trading in TDRs
(v) Loan amount credited to the NRO account of the NRI/PIO
(vi) Loan amount shall NOT be remitted outside India

=============================================================================
DEFINITION OF "PERSON RESIDENT IN INDIA" (Section 2(v), FEMA 1999)
=============================================================================

A person residing in India for more than 182 days during the course of the preceding financial year
BUT does NOT include:
(A) A person who has gone out of India or stays outside India for:
    (a) Employment outside India
    (b) Carrying on business/vocation outside India
    (c) Any other purpose indicating intention to stay outside India for an uncertain period

(B) A person who has come to or stays in India for:
    (a) Employment in India
    (b) Carrying on business/vocation in India
    (c) Any other purpose indicating intention to stay in India for an uncertain period

Also included:
(ii) Any person or body corporate registered or incorporated in India
(iii) An office, branch or agency in India owned or controlled by a person resident outside India
(iv) An office, branch or agency outside India owned or controlled by a person resident in India

=============================================================================
ADDITIONAL CONTEXT: LRS AND GIFT CITY IFSC
=============================================================================

GIFT City IFSC (Gujarat International Finance Tec-City International Financial Services Centre):
- IFSC entities are treated as "persons resident outside India" under FEMA 1999
- Therefore, remittances to GIFT City IFSC funds = LRS transactions
- Subject to USD 250,000 per person per FY limit
- Subject to TCS under Section 206C(1G) of the Income Tax Act
- Section 206C(1G)(a) was amended to remove "out of India" to specifically include GIFT City

This means:
- Resident individual investing in PPFAS IFSC, Tata S&P 500 IFSC, Mirae IFSC, etc. must use LRS
- TCS applies: 0% up to ₹10L per PAN per FY, 20% above (for investment purpose)
- The ₹10L threshold is tracked per PAN across ALL banks via CIMS (from Jan 2026)
- Family members each have independent ₹10L TCS-free buckets under their own PANs
- Form A2 must be filed for each remittance

KEY ADVANTAGE OF GIFT CITY IFSC (beyond LRS scope):
- Once remitted, gains can be reinvested within IFSC without fresh LRS remittance
- Zero STT, zero stamp duty, zero GST on IFSC transactions
- No SEBI $7B overseas investment cap (unlike INR-route domestic feeder funds)
- US estate tax eliminated by holding via Ireland UCITS ETF wrappers
