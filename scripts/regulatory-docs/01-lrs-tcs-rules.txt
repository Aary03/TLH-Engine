LIBERALISED REMITTANCE SCHEME (LRS) AND TAX COLLECTED AT SOURCE (TCS)
Indian Income Tax Act and RBI Master Directions
Effective: April 1, 2025 (Budget 2025 amendments)

=============================================================================
SECTION 1: LRS OVERVIEW
=============================================================================

The Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS) permits each resident individual to 
remit up to USD 250,000 per financial year for permissible current or capital 
account transactions under FEMA 1999.

Key points:
- Limit: USD 250,000 per resident individual per financial year (April–March)
- Each adult family member has their own independent USD 250,000 limit
- Minors are eligible with natural guardian signing Form A2
- Transactions tracked via Form A2 at Authorized Dealer (AD) Category-I/II banks

Permissible purposes under LRS:
- Investment in overseas securities, mutual funds, ETFs
- Investment in GIFT City IFSC funds (treated as foreign under FEMA)
- Education (self-funded or with education loan)
- Medical treatment abroad
- Overseas tour packages
- Maintenance of close relatives abroad
- Gifts to non-residents

=============================================================================
SECTION 2: TCS RATES AND THRESHOLDS (Budget 2025 Amendment)
=============================================================================

Section 206C(1G) of the Income Tax Act governs TCS on LRS remittances.

TCS THRESHOLD: Rs. 10 lakh (Rs. 10,00,000) per PAN per financial year
This threshold applies to the TOTAL of ALL LRS purposes combined, tracked per PAN.

TCS RATE TABLE (Effective April 1, 2025):

Purpose                               | Up to Rs. 10L  | Above Rs. 10L
--------------------------------------|----------------|---------------
Education (loan from Sec 80E inst.)   | 0%             | 0%
Education (self-funded)               | 0%             | 5%
Medical treatment abroad              | 0%             | 5%
Overseas tour packages                | 5% (from Re 1) | 20%
All other purposes (investment,       | 0%             | 20%
gifts, maintenance, GIFT City funds)  |                |

IMPORTANT NOTE: Air tickets and hotel bookings processed as LRS by banks 
fall under "any other purpose" (0% up to Rs. 10L) — NOT tour packages (5%).
This is a meaningful optimization for travelers.

GIFT CITY IFSC REMITTANCES: Section 206C(1G)(a) was amended to omit "out of India"
specifically to ensure TCS applies to GIFT City IFSC remittances. GIFT City entities
are "persons resident outside India" under FEMA, so remittances to them via LRS
attract TCS at the same rates as any other investment remittance.

=============================================================================
SECTION 3: CALCULATION EXAMPLES
=============================================================================

Example 1: Rs. 80 lakh investment remittance (single PAN)
- First Rs. 10 lakh: 0% TCS = Rs. 0
- Next Rs. 70 lakh: 20% TCS = Rs. 14 lakh
- Total TCS = Rs. 14 lakh

Example 2: Rs. 80 lakh split across 4 family members
- Each remits Rs. 20 lakh
- Each: Rs. 10 lakh at 0% + Rs. 10 lakh at 20% = Rs. 2 lakh per person
- Total TCS = Rs. 8 lakh (vs Rs. 14 lakh without splitting = Rs. 6 lakh saved)

Example 3: Cross-FY strategy
- Rs. 10 lakh before March 31 (FY end): 0% TCS
- Rs. 10 lakh after April 1 (new FY): 0% TCS
- Total Rs. 20 lakh at 0% TCS = Rs. 0 TCS paid
- vs Rs. 2 lakh TCS if remitted in same FY (Rs. 10L above threshold × 20%)

=============================================================================
SECTION 4: TCS CREDIT AND REFUND
=============================================================================

TCS appears in Form 26AS (Part F) within days of bank deposit.
Claim in ITR under "Taxes Paid → TCS Details" with Collector TAN.
Refund typically processes in 7–45 days post e-verification of ITR.

OPPORTUNITY COST OF TCS:
At 20% TCS on Rs. 1 crore above threshold:
- Rs. 20 lakh locked for 6–15 months until ITR refund
- At 12% expected returns: ~Rs. 2.4 lakh opportunity cost
- Mitigation: offset TCS against advance tax installments (June 15, Sep 15, Dec 15, Mar 15)

=============================================================================
SECTION 5: CIMS TRACKING (From January 1, 2026)
=============================================================================

Since January 1, 2026, RBI's Centralised Information Management System (CIMS)
enables real-time cross-bank PAN-wise cumulative LRS tracking via mandatory 
daily returns from all AD Category-I/II banks and FFMCs.

Self-declaration alone no longer suffices. All LRS remittances are tracked 
in real-time across all banks by PAN. Undisclosed or duplicate declarations
are detectable immediately.

=============================================================================
SECTION 6: FAMILY SPLITTING RULES
=============================================================================

Each adult resident individual gets their own Rs. 10 lakh TCS-free threshold.
A family of 4 adults can collectively remit up to USD 1,000,000/year with
Rs. 40 lakh completely TCS-free.

Requirements for valid family splitting:
1. Each remitter must independently fill Form A2
2. Each must provide their own valid PAN
3. Funds must genuinely belong to the remitter (not artificially transferred)
4. Each family member must have independent income/assets to justify the remittance

GAAR (General Anti-Avoidance Rules) considerations: Legitimate family 
splitting for genuine investment purposes is fully legal. Artificial 
arrangements without commercial substance could attract GAAR scrutiny.
