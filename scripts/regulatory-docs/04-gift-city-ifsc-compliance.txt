GIFT CITY IFSC COMPLIANCE — SCHEDULE FA, FSI, FORM 67
Income Tax Act, FEMA, Black Money Act

=============================================================================
SECTION 1: WHY GIFT CITY IFSC IS TREATED AS FOREIGN
=============================================================================

Despite being physically located in India (Gandhinagar, Gujarat), 
GIFT City IFSC entities are treated as "persons resident outside India" 
under FEMA 1999.

This creates important compliance obligations:

1. Remittances to GIFT City IFSC funds = LRS transactions (attract TCS)
2. Holdings in GIFT City IFSC funds = FOREIGN ASSETS for disclosure purposes
3. Schedule FA of ITR must be filled (not optional)
4. CRS/FATCA reporting may apply

Section 206C(1G)(a) was specifically amended to omit "out of India" to ensure
TCS applies to GIFT City remittances. Parliament explicitly intended GIFT City
funds to be treated as foreign investments for TCS purposes.

=============================================================================
SECTION 2: SCHEDULE FA — FOREIGN ASSET DISCLOSURE
=============================================================================

WHO MUST FILE SCHEDULE FA:
Any resident individual or HUF holding foreign assets during the relevant year.
This INCLUDES GIFT City IFSC fund holdings.

WHEN TO USE:
- ITR-2: For individuals with no business income
- ITR-3: For individuals with business/professional income
- ITR-1 and ITR-4 CANNOT be used if you have foreign assets

WHAT TO DISCLOSE (per account/investment):
- Country code: IN (India — since IFSC is in India, though treated as foreign under FEMA)
- Name of institution/AMC
- Account/folio number
- Opening balance (January 1 of calendar year)
- Peak balance during the year
- Closing balance (December 31 of calendar year)
- INR equivalent at SBI TTBR rate

REPORTING PERIOD: CALENDAR YEAR (January 1 – December 31)
This differs from the Indian financial year (April 1 – March 31).
Schedule FA uses calendar year reporting even when filed in ITR for Indian FY.

PENALTY FOR NON-DISCLOSURE:
Under the Black Money (Undisclosed Foreign Income and Assets) and 
Imposition of Tax Act, 2015:
- Penalty: Rs. 10 LAKH per year of non-disclosure
- This applies even if the income is otherwise properly reported in ITR
- Prosecution: 6 months to 7 YEARS imprisonment for willful non-disclosure
- The penalty is per asset, per year — multiple years × multiple assets = severe penalties

BUDGET 2024 RELIEF: No penalty for non-immovable foreign assets below 
AGGREGATE value of Rs. 20 lakh. This provides some relief for small holdings.

=============================================================================
SECTION 3: SCHEDULE FSI — FOREIGN SOURCE INCOME
=============================================================================

Schedule FSI captures all income from sources outside India, including:
- Dividends from IFSC funds
- Interest from IFSC instruments
- Capital gains on IFSC fund redemptions

Each income item must show:
- Country of source
- Income amount in foreign currency
- INR equivalent
- Tax paid in foreign country (for FTC calculation)
- Applicable DTAA

=============================================================================
SECTION 4: SCHEDULE TR — TAX RELIEF CLAIMED
=============================================================================

Schedule TR summarizes tax relief claimed under:
- Section 90 (DTAA relief)
- Section 91 (unilateral relief for non-DTAA countries)

For each relief:
- Country of income source
- Tax paid in foreign country
- Foreign tax credit claimed
- Method: Exemption or Credit

=============================================================================
SECTION 5: FORM 67 — FOREIGN TAX CREDIT
=============================================================================

Form 67 must be filed ELECTRONICALLY to claim Foreign Tax Credit (FTC) 
under Section 90/91 of the IT Act.

DEADLINE: End of assessment year (March 31 of AY) — per CBDT Notification 100/2022
Example: For FY 2025-26 → File Form 67 by March 31, 2027

WHAT'S NEEDED:
- Certificate from foreign tax authority OR broker confirmation of WHT
- Proof of tax paid in foreign country
- Self-attestation
- Currency conversion at SBI TTBR on last day of preceding month

FTC CALCULATION:
FTC = Lower of:
(a) Tax paid in foreign country on specific income
(b) Indian tax on that specific income (computed proportionately)

COMMON MISTAKES IN FORM 67:
1. Late filing (after March 31 of AY) — FTC claim denied
2. Mismatch between Form 67 and ITR — triggers notice
3. Wrong currency conversion date (must be last day of preceding month)
4. Claiming FTC on disputed/contested taxes (not allowed)

=============================================================================
SECTION 6: CONSISTENCY REQUIREMENT
=============================================================================

ALL FOUR FILINGS must be INTERNALLY CONSISTENT:
- Schedule FA (foreign assets)
- Schedule FSI (foreign source income)
- Schedule TR (tax relief)
- Form 67 (foreign tax credit)

Mismatches between these four trigger automatic compliance notices from 
the Income Tax Department.

The Department also receives CRS/FATCA data from foreign financial institutions
and will independently know about undisclosed foreign holdings.
