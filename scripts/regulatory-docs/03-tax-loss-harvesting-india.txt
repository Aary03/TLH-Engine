TAX LOSS HARVESTING IN INDIA — NO WASH SALE RULES
Income Tax Act 1961 — Sections 70, 71, 72, 74, 94(7), 94(8)

=============================================================================
SECTION 1: INDIA HAS NO WASH SALE RULES
=============================================================================

CRITICAL FACT: India's Income Tax Act does NOT have wash sale rules 
equivalent to Section 1091 of the US Internal Revenue Code.

In the United States:
- If you sell a security at a loss, you cannot buy the "substantially identical" 
  security within 30 days before or after the sale
- Violation results in disallowance of the loss for tax purposes

In India:
- NO such 30-day or 61-day disallowance period exists
- NO "substantially identical securities" concept
- An investor can sell a security at a loss and IMMEDIATELY rebuy the same 
  security on the SAME DAY
- The loss is fully recognized for tax purposes
- The new cost basis is the repurchase price (stepped down)

This has been confirmed by:
- Zerodha Tax P&L reports
- ClearTax guidance
- Angel One tax documentation
- Multiple chartered accountant interpretations
- No CBDT notification has created any wash sale equivalent

=============================================================================
SECTION 2: GAAR CONSIDERATIONS
=============================================================================

GAAR (General Anti-Avoidance Rules, Chapter X-A of the IT Act, effective April 2017)
can theoretically deny tax benefits to transactions lacking commercial substance.

In practice:
- Genuine market transactions at market prices on recognized exchanges are SAFE
- The key risk boundary: avoid CIRCULAR trading with NO change in position 
  purely for loss generation
- Example of SAFE practice: Sell Fund A at a loss, immediately rebuy Fund A.
  New cost basis established. Transaction is at market prices. Fully legal.
- Example of risky practice: Prearranged circular trades between related 
  parties purely to generate paper losses without any economic substance

TAX LOSS HARVESTING at market prices with genuine securities is explicitly 
considered a legitimate tax planning strategy — not tax evasion.

=============================================================================
SECTION 3: SET-OFF AND CARRY-FORWARD RULES
=============================================================================

Section 70: Intra-head set-off (within capital gains)
Section 71: Inter-head set-off (between different heads)
Section 72: Carry-forward of business losses
Section 74: Carry-forward of capital losses

SET-OFF TABLE:
Loss Type | Can Offset Against
STCL      | STCG (current year) AND LTCG (current year) — BOTH types
LTCL      | LTCG ONLY — CANNOT offset STCG

STCL is SUPERIOR because:
- STCL has broader offset capability
- STCL offsets STCG at up to 42.74%
- STCL offsets LTCG at up to 14.95%
- LTCL can only offset LTCG at 14.95%

CAPITAL LOSSES CANNOT OFFSET:
- Salary income
- House property income
- Business/professional income
- Other sources income

CARRY-FORWARD RULES:
- Duration: 8 ASSESSMENT YEARS from year of loss
- Both STCL and LTCL can be carried forward
- CRITICAL: ITR must be filed ON OR BEFORE the due date (typically July 31)
  to preserve carry-forward rights
- Belated ITR filing = LOSS OF CARRY-FORWARD RIGHTS PERMANENTLY
- Even if income is below taxable limit, file ITR to preserve carry-forwards

INTRA-YEAR ORDER OF SET-OFF:
1. Intra-head set-off first (STCL vs STCG within capital gains)
2. Then STCL vs LTCG
3. Then LTCL vs LTCG
4. Remaining unabsorbed losses carry forward

=============================================================================
SECTION 4: SECTION 94(8) — BONUS STRIPPING (The Only Wash-Sale-Like Rule)
=============================================================================

Section 94(8) of the IT Act disallows losses on mutual fund units in a 
specific bonus stripping scenario:

CONDITIONS FOR DISALLOWANCE:
1. Investor acquires MF units within 3 months BEFORE the record date
2. Receives bonus units on the record date
3. Sells the ORIGINAL units at a loss within 9 months AFTER the record date

EFFECT: The loss on original units is ignored. It is deemed to be the 
cost of the bonus units received.

This is NARROWLY targeted at bonus stripping. It is NOT a general wash sale 
provision and does NOT apply to:
- Normal tax loss harvesting without bonus units
- Selling and rebuying without any bonus units involved
- Any other scenario not involving MF bonus units

SECTION 5: THE TLH ALGORITHM FOR INDIAN INVESTORS
=============================================================================

Priority ranking (highest tax savings to lowest):
1. STCL offsetting EXISTING STCG → saves 42.74% (highest value)
2. STCL offsetting EXISTING LTCG → saves 14.95%
3. LTCL offsetting EXISTING LTCG → saves 14.95%
4. STCL for carry-forward → future savings (8 years)
5. LTCL for carry-forward → limited future use (only vs LTCG)

CALCULATION:
net_benefit = (loss_amount × effective_tax_rate) - transaction_costs

Where transaction_costs include:
- Brokerage commission (typically 0.05–0.15%)
- Bid-ask spread
- Exit loads (if applicable for MF)
- Impact cost (for large trades)

MINIMUM THRESHOLD: Generally Rs. 10,000–50,000 in net benefit to justify execution.
For HNI portfolios with high STCG exposure, even smaller losses may be worth harvesting.

TIMING STRATEGY:
- January–March: MOST CRITICAL window (final opportunity before March 31)
- March 25–31: Last-chance trades (ensure settlement before March 31)
- Post-budget July–August: Volatility creates opportunities
- Any market correction: Opportunistic harvesting regardless of calendar

Continuous weekly harvesting is superior to year-end-only harvesting because:
- Losses present in Q1-Q2 may disappear by Q4 if markets recover
- Earlier harvesting = earlier tax benefit
- Spread execution across the year avoids market impact

=============================================================================
SECTION 6: NEW COST BASIS AND DOCUMENTATION
=============================================================================

After tax loss harvesting:
1. Old units sold at market price → STCL/LTCL recognized
2. New units purchased at market price → new (lower) cost basis established
3. New holding period RESTARTS from purchase date
4. The new cost basis is the repurchase price (not the original cost)

DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED:
- Broker trade confirmations for sale and rebuy
- Statement showing old cost basis and new cost basis
- Capital gains statement from broker/AMC
- Portfolio statement showing lot-level details
- CA sign-off for large harvesting operations (recommended)

AUDIT TRAIL: Maintain clear records showing:
- Business purpose: Establishing lower cost basis for future gains reduction
- Market prices used for both buy and sell
- No circular trading with related parties
- Genuine change in cost basis achieved
